# Code of Conduct

LoginGuard is committed to a welcoming, respectful, and professional open-source community.

## Expected Behavior

Participants are expected to:

- Be respectful and constructive.
- Welcome beginners and answer questions in good faith.
- Keep discussions focused on defensive, educational, and authorized security work.
- Give and receive feedback with patience.
- Respect privacy and avoid sharing sensitive information.

## Unacceptable Behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- Harassment, insults, threats, or discrimination.
- Sexualized or violent language.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Encouraging unauthorized testing or misuse.
- Sharing offensive payloads or instructions for attacking third-party systems.
- Repeated disruption of project discussions.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove comments, close issues, reject contributions, or restrict participation when behavior conflicts with this code of conduct.

If you need to report a conduct issue, contact the maintainers through the project repository. Include relevant links, screenshots, or context when safe to do so.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to project spaces, including issues, pull requests, documentation, discussions, and community channels connected to LoginGuard.
